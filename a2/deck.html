Explainability · 2 of 3

Where CareBridge sits in the explainability taxonomy

Data-based (pre-model)

Data document: missingness, leakage, training-bias, recency audits. Policy version-pinning = data-lineage explainability.

Model-based (in-model)

Deliberately ante-hoc: RAG grounding + per-criterion structure makes reasoning inspectable by construction — we don't bolt SHAP onto a black box; we constrained the box.

Results-based (post-model)

The packet: citations, statuses, confidence, rationale; counterfactual is native — "what flips this pend to approve" = the named missing item.
Corner cases (the brief asks): conflicting notes → criterion surfaces BOTH spans, statused conflict, routed to MD; missing policy version → hard fail to human, never silent fallback; low retrieval confidence → packet declares evidence-completeness "medium/low" instead of bluffing. All three visible in the demo.

The question you should ask us

"This looks polished. Is it still a prototype?" — Yes. Here is why.

  • Synthetic data only. No PHI, no auth, no persistence, no EHR integration, no live PA touched.
  • Vibe-coded and disposable — the demo is throwaway; the fidelity lives in the explanation surface (citations, statuses, audit trail), because that is the hypothesis being sold, not the software.
  • It still tests falsifiable claims: packet → faster review (touch-time), packet → trustable (groundedness). It ignores latency, scaling, MLOps — those are the MVP build, priced separately.
  • A1 discipline unchanged: fail fast, $35K gate before $1.6M build.
Polish in the explanation is the product hypothesis. Polish in the engineering would be waste — so there isn't any.
