Slide 1 of record — the boardroom summary

Executive summary — everything a decision-maker needs, on one slide

The problem

650K prior-auths/yr; ~390K need a licensed UM nurse. She spends her touch-time assembling evidence, not deciding. $4.10M/yr of reviewer labor sits on that assembly.

The intervention

A RAG-grounded AI decision packet: every policy criterion marked met / unmet / uncertain, every assertion cited to its source span. AI can approve-recommend, pend, or route — it structurally cannot deny.

The evidence (A1 prototype)

500-case crossover test, 6 RN reviewers, ~$35K, 4 weeks. Primary endpoint: touch-time −35% (kill <25%). Hard stop: groundedness ≥95%. Gates a $1.6M build at 2.5% of its cost.

The economics

Net annual value ~$0.55M (mid case) to ~$1.5M (upside), band honest: near break-even at the pessimistic corner. AI run cost ≈ $0.15–0.20/case against $10.50 of labor it compresses.
The ask: approve MVP scoping conditional on the prototype gates — infrastructure, security, responsibility and explainability designs on the following slides are the plan for that MVP.

Mandatory · application summary

What CareBridge is — the 7-step workflow

  1. PA request lands (X12 278/portal); deterministic layer confirms eligibility, codes, benefit — rules/RPA, deliberately not AI.
  2. AI retrieves patient evidence + the version-pinned medical-necessity policy.
  3. AI maps evidence → each criterion, citing the exact source span.
  4. AI assembles the packet + recommendation (approve / pend-for-info / route-for-review).
  5. Nurse opens packet, verifies citations, decides. The only step we compress.
  6. Denials route to the medical director — a licensed human owns every adverse outcome.
  7. Every assertion, citation and human action → immutable audit log.

Primary: UM nurseSecondary: medical director / exceptionsTertiary: UM ops executive (read-only dashboard)

Explainability · 2 of 3

Where CareBridge sits in the explainability taxonomy

Data-based (pre-model)

Data document: missingness, leakage, training-bias, recency audits. Policy version-pinning = data-lineage explainability.

Model-based (in-model)

Deliberately ante-hoc: RAG grounding + per-criterion structure makes the reasoning inspectable by construction — we don't bolt SHAP onto a black box; we constrained the box.

Results-based (post-model)

The packet: citations, statuses, confidence, rationale; counterfactual is native — "what would flip this pend to approve" = the named missing item.
Corner cases (the brief asks): conflicting notes → criterion surfaces BOTH spans, statused uncertain, routed; missing policy version → hard fail to human, never silent fallback; low retrieval confidence → packet declares evidence-completeness "medium/low" instead of bluffing. Demonstrated live in the demo.

Infrastructure · 1 of 2

CPU or GPU? The latency budget decides — and it's hours, not milliseconds

Prototype

CPU-only, batch. Retrospective cases, overnight job. Zero real-time requirement. (The brief's own point: CPU inference is the cost lever when latency permits.)

MVP — hybrid

On-prem GPU only for PHI-side embedding/retrieval; managed LLM API for de-identified criterion reasoning; rules/RPA for the deterministic 60–70%. Queue-ahead ("delayed") processing: packet ready before the nurse opens the case.
Standard PA turnaround: 3.5 days. Paying for millisecond GPU serving against a multi-day SLA is buying speed nobody asked for.

Responsible AI · 1 of 2

Productivity gain without replacement — the engagement design

  • Gain: 35–45% touch-time reduction → $1.43M/yr gross at the floor → realized as capacity release: assembly minutes become judgment minutes; TAT 3.5 days → <1 day on clean cases.
  • Enabler, not replacer — by structure, not slogan: the AI cannot deny. Its ceiling is pend/route. The licensed human's authority is a design constraint, not a policy promise.
  • Engagement mechanics: reviewers co-design packet layout; a disagreement button on every packet (her override is training signal, not insubordination); savings published back as capacity, not quota; judgment-ladder career path (complex cases, audits, appeals).

The question you should ask us

"This looks polished. Is it still a prototype?" — Yes. Here is why.

  • Synthetic data only. No PHI, no auth, no persistence, no EHR integration, no live PA touched.
  • Vibe-coded and disposable — the demo is throwaway; the fidelity lives in the explanation surface (citations, statuses, audit trail), because that is the hypothesis being sold, not the software.
  • It still tests falsifiable claims: packet → faster review (touch-time), packet → trustable (groundedness). It ignores latency, scaling, MLOps — those are the MVP build, priced separately.
  • A1 discipline unchanged: fail fast, $35K gate before $1.6M build.
Polish in the explanation is the product hypothesis. Polish in the engineering would be waste — so there isn't any.
