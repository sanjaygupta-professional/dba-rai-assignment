# Course Title: Responsible AI: Building AI Systems That Truly Deliver Value

> **Course Overview**

Responsible AI is not merely about ticking compliance checklists or
following abstract ethical guidelines. It is about building AI systems
that deliver real-world impact---technically sound, economically viable,

ethically aligned, and operationally scalable.

This program is built around *DARWIN*, a proprietary framework developed
to guide AI product development from conception to

deployment. Participants will learn to translate business challenges
into robust AI solutions, address bias and infrastructure decisions,
define

stakeholder-centric metrics, and create explainable, observable, and
sustainable AI workflows.

By the end of the program, participants will be equipped to design a
comprehensive blueprint for successful AI product development,

regardless of domain or coding background.

# Learning Outcomes

Participants will learn to:

- Translate a business problem into a feasible AI product plan.

- Design rapid AI prototypes and evolve them into full-scale products.

- Identify and address data and model biases using structured feature
  engineering.

- Evaluate infrastructure options (cloud, open source, compute
  resources) and green performance metrics.

- Define team roles, understand global compliance, and conduct economic
  feasibility analysis.

- Align workflows with stakeholder needs using explainability,
  performance metrics, and observability.

# Note:

No prior coding knowledge is required

. Additional resources such as research papers, slides, and tutorials
will be provided throughout the program.

# Evaluation Criteria

> Evaluation will be based on **two team presentations** made during the
> course, with the following guidelines:

- Teams must be formed by the end of the first week.

- All team members will receive equal grades **unless** it is explicitly
  communicated that a member did not contribute.

- Each team member must present in at least **one** of the two rounds.
